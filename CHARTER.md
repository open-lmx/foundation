# Open-LMX Foundation Charter

## Purpose

Open-LMX Foundation exists to define an open, interoperable, governable foundation for AI-native systems.

The project focuses on shared language, schemas, lifecycle models, governance rules, trust mechanisms, and certification patterns needed to operate agents, models, tools, skills, workflows, knowledge, memory, and AI systems across organizations and environments.

## Vision

A world where intelligent systems can safely collaborate with humans, organizations, applications, and other agents through open standards, transparent governance, verifiable identity, and auditable execution.

## Mission

To create the foundational standards for AI-native interoperability, governance, evaluation, and trust.

## Scope

Open-LMX Foundation covers:

- Asset metadata
- Identity models
- Relationship models
- Lifecycle states
- Trust scoring
- Evaluation models
- Policy models
- Governance controls
- Certification frameworks
- Interoperability profiles
- Reference architecture

## Out of Scope

Open-LMX Foundation does not directly provide:

- A commercial enterprise platform
- Managed cloud hosting
- Proprietary model services
- Vendor-specific lock-in
- Closed governance processes

These may be implemented by downstream projects.

## Compatibility

Open-LMX Foundation should align with open standards where possible, including JSON Schema, JSON-LD, schema.org, OpenAPI, AsyncAPI, OpenTelemetry, OPA, OpenFGA, MCP, A2A, DID, Verifiable Credentials, and CNCF cloud-native practices.

## Specification Maturity

Specifications must clearly declare maturity:

- Draft
- Candidate
- Approved
- Deprecated
- Retired

## North Star Metric

Trusted value delivered per unit cost per unit time while remaining compliant.

# Open-LMX Governance

## Governance Purpose

Governance exists to keep Open-LMX stable, transparent, interoperable, and trustworthy.

Open-LMX governance applies to principles, schemas, specifications, examples, reference models, conformance rules, and certification paths.

## Governance Hierarchy

```text
Law
Safety
Mission
Principles
Policies
Standards
Controls
Objectives
Tasks
Actions
```

A lower layer cannot override a higher layer.

## Change Model

All meaningful changes should be traceable through proposals, issues, commits, pull requests, reviews, and release notes.

Changes may affect:

- Charter
- Principles
- Schemas
- Specifications
- Examples
- Governance rules
- Compatibility requirements
- Certification requirements

## Specification States

Each specification must declare one of the following states:

- Draft
- Candidate
- Approved
- Deprecated
- Retired

## Decision Requirements

Every major decision should capture:

- Context
- Options considered
- Decision made
- Rationale
- Risks
- Reviewers
- Approval status
- Effective date

## Review Rules

Schema and governance changes require review before being marked approved.

Reviews should check:

- Interoperability
- Security
- Privacy
- Portability
- Compatibility
- Operational impact
- Backward compatibility
- Documentation completeness

## Compatibility Rule

Approved specifications should not introduce breaking changes without a migration path.

## Evidence Rule

Claims about reliability, safety, trust, performance, or maturity must be supported by evidence.

## Human Accountability

Automated systems may propose, validate, and reconcile changes, but accountability remains with human maintainers and approved governance bodies.

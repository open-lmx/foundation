# Open-LMX Career Graph Model

## Purpose

The Career Graph defines how human capability evolves over time across roles, jobs, and industries.

It is the temporal layer that connects capability to long-term growth, progression, and opportunity.

---

## Core Insight

> Capability is static unless placed in time.
> Career is capability evolving through structured work.

---

## Career Graph Entities

- CareerPath
- CareerStage
- CareerLevel
- PromotionEvent

- CapabilityGrowth

- DevelopmentPlan
- MentorshipRelation

---

## Career Lifecycle

### 1. Entry Phase
- onboarding into role
- baseline capability assessment

### 2. Growth Phase
- skill expansion
- competency strengthening
- performance stabilization

### 3. Acceleration Phase
- role transitions
- increased responsibility
- capability amplification

### 4. Leadership Phase
- mentoring others
- defining roles
- influencing systems

## Promotion Model

A promotion is not a title change.

It is a verified transition in capability thresholds:

- increased responsibility scope
- improved performance consistency
- validated evidence accumulation

---

## Key Principle

> Careers are not job sequences.
> Careers are capability trajectories over time.

---

## Trust Linkage

Career progression must be validated through:

- employment graph evidence
- social graph validation
- trust graph scoring

---

## Final Role of Career Graph

The Career Graph provides temporal meaning to all other graphs:

- Capability becomes measurable over time
- Roles become progression points
- Work becomes structured experience
- Learning becomes trajectory-driven

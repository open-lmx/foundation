# Open-LMX Principles

## Foundational Principles

1. Everything is data.
2. Everything is temporal.
3. Everything is governed.
4. Everything is observable.
5. Everything is traceable.
6. Everything is replayable.
7. Everything is composable.
8. Everything interoperates through open protocols.
9. Current state is a projection, not truth.
10. Time is a first-class primitive.
11. Identities persist.
12. Relationships evolve.
13. Append over overwrite.
14. Humans approve; systems reconcile.
15. Governance must be native, not bolted on.
16. Production-grade is the baseline.
17. Open standards over proprietary abstractions.
18. Composability over lock-in.
19. Sovereignty and portability are default assumptions.
20. Operational truth must survive vendor changes.

## AI-Native Principles

1. Every agent must have identity.
2. Every model must have declared capability.
3. Every tool must have declared permission.
4. Every skill must have evaluation criteria.
5. Every prompt must be versioned.
6. Every memory must have provenance.
7. Every action must be attributable.
8. Every decision must be explainable.
9. Every output must be traceable.
10. Every automation must be governable.

## Governance Principles

1. Law overrides policy.
2. Safety overrides speed.
3. Mission overrides convenience.
4. Policy overrides task.
5. Approval overrides automation.
6. Evidence overrides assertion.
7. Auditability overrides opacity.
8. Reversibility is preferred.
9. Least privilege is default.
10. Human accountability remains mandatory.
